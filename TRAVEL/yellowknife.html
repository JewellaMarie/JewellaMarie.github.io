Welcome To YellowKnife



Why Yellowknife is Special to Me

Yellowknife whispers of a celestial bodies, a dream etched in my heart since childhood. It's the stage where the Aurora Borealis, nature's own light show, paints the night sky with colors I've only seen in movies. This isn't just a place to look at the lights; it's a doorway to that dream. Away from city glare, the sky is a clear canvas for the dancing colors. Imagine standing beneath it, feeling the crisp air, and watch the lights flicker and dance, a sight that's both ethereal and humbling. But Yellowknife is more than just a light show. It's a winter playground. Think snowmobiling across frozen lakes. For me, seeing the Aurora is like finally healing a childhood dream, for this is something the child in me always wished upon. Yellowknife offers that chance to not just tick off a bucket list item, but to feel that childlike wonder again, to be part of nature's grand story. It's a chance to step into a real-life fairytale.


How to Get There

I am no good with directions but based on research, Traveling from Quezon City to Yellowknife requires taking connecting flights from Manila's Ninoy Aquino International Airport (MNL) to Yellowknife Airport (YZF). Expect at least one stop, often in a major Canadian city, resulting in a total travel time of 17 to over 30 hours. Flight costs can vary widely, ranging from roughly PHP 52,000 to PHP 180,000 or more. Booking in advance and being flexible with travel dates can help find better deals. As a Filipino citizen, a Canadian visa will likely be necessary, so apply well ahead of your trip. Upon arrival in Yellowknife, transportation to downtown includes taxis, hotel shuttles, public buses, and car rentals. Be aware of the significant 14-hour time difference.


Other Places to Visit in Yellowknife

  • Old Town - Explore the historic heart of Yellowknife with its colorful houses and unique architecture.
  • Prince of Wales Northern Heritage Centre - Learn about the history and culture of the Northwest Territories.
  • Great Slave Lake - One of the largest and deepest lakes in the world, perfect for fishing and ice activities.
  • Ice Road - Drive on the famous ice roads during winter for a unique experience.
  • Snowking's Winter Festival - A magical snow castle event held annually in March.
